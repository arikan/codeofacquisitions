About

Code of Acquisitions is a platform exposing both good and bad practices of art institutions, galleries, and studios. It is based on published policies as well as reported cases of misconduct and abuse. Instances include artists not being paid, being misled post-sales, or not having their works returned.

Often, artists find it challenging to pursue legal action against a conflict due to their precarious conditions, and particularly because lack of establishing individual legal proceedings overseas.

The Code of Acquisitions platform allows anyone to submit their cases, either anonymously or openly. Cases could range from gender and race discrimination to misconduct of payments.

Workshops

We conduct workshops upon invitation from institutions. During these sessions, participants propose cases based on published sources, collectively evaluate them, and add them to the database.

Methodology

1. Submissions

Submitted cases are collected through an open form into a database and prioritized based on the provided information. A committee of fact-checkers evaluate the cases.

2. Assignment

Committee members periodically view and assign themselves to cases based on their expertise.

3. Research & Analysis

Assigned members conduct research and mark cases as FAIL, SUCCEED, COMPLEX, or UNCERTAIN.

4. Publishing

Cases undergo secondary review before being published in the database.

5. Impact & Updates

Status updates are made when institutions address and resolve issues.

Principles

Commitment to Fairness

We conduct research on all sides of the issue and follow the same process for every fact check.

Transparency of Sources

We provide detailed sources for verification while protecting personal security when needed.

Funding & Organization

We ensure funders have no influence over fact-checkers' conclusions.

Transparent Methodology

We explain our methods for selecting, researching, writing, editing, and publishing fact checks.

Open Corrections

We correct clearly and transparently to ensure readers can see the corrected version.
